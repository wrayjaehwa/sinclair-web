On a certain October evening in 1926, the lamps of Sinclair House burn a little brighter. The liquor is questionable, the company even more so. By dessert, their host, J.W. Sinclair, will be dead.

This private index has been prepared for those with sufficient curiosity – or sufficient debt – to require a closer look at the man, his guests, and his arrangements.

Vault

Behind one of these pages lies the combination to the wall safe in Sinclair’s study. Only those who have untangled his paper trail should dare to open it.

Characters

Rivals, bootleggers, family, and staff – each with a debt owed to Sinclair in coin, favor, or blood. Their names and roles are recorded for convenient reference.

Menu

A modest bill of fare: ribs and wings, scampi and macaroni, salad for the cautious, and enough drink to loosen tongues and cloud judgments.

“A man is only as secure as the stories his guests are afraid to tell.” – J.W. Sinclair
